## Licensing and Insurance Requirements in North Dakota

North Dakota requires any contractor performing work valued at $4,000 or more to hold a state contractor license issued through the North Dakota Secretary of State Contractor Licensing Division. The license must be renewed annually and tied to a surety bond.

Before you hire any roofer, ask for these documents in writing:

| Requirement | What to Verify |
|---|---|
| ND contractor license | Active number with the Secretary of State |
| General liability insurance | Minimum $1 million coverage certificate |
| Workers' compensation | Active policy through ND Workforce Safety & Insurance |
| Surety bond | Tied to current ND license class |

Highpoint Construction carries an active North Dakota contractor license, $1M+ general liability coverage, and full workers' comp through ND WSI. We provide certificates of insurance directly to homeowners and insurance adjusters on request.

## Certifications and Manufacturer Partnerships

Manufacturer certifications matter because they unlock extended warranties unavailable to uncertified contractors. The two most rigorous programs in residential roofing are GAF Master Elite (held by roughly the top 2% of U.S. roofers) and CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster. Both require proof of licensing, insurance, ongoing training, and a clean complaint record.

Highpoint Construction maintains active manufacturer credentials with leading shingle and metal roofing brands used across North Dakota. Ask us which certification applies to the product you choose — the answer affects your warranty.

## Warranty Coverage: Materials and Workmanship

A real roofing warranty has two parts:

- **Manufacturer warranty** — covers defects in shingles, underlayment, or metal panels (typically 25 years to lifetime).
- **Workmanship warranty** — covers installation errors made by the contractor.

The NRCA recommends a minimum 5-year workmanship warranty on residential roof replacement. Highpoint Construction provides a written workmanship warranty on every roof replacement project in Bismarck ND and surrounding markets, separate from the manufacturer's coverage. The terms are spelled out on your contract — never verbal, never vague.

## Transparent Estimates and Contract Process

A trustworthy roofer puts every number on paper. Your written estimate should list:

- Tear-off and disposal scope
- Decking inspection and replacement rate per sheet
- Underlayment type and brand
- Shingle or metal panel product line and color
- Flashing, drip edge, and ventilation details
- Total price with payment schedule
- Start window and estimated completion

Highpoint Construction provides itemized estimates with no pressure tactics, no "today only" discounts, and no deposits beyond what state law allows. You receive the full scope in writing before any work begins.

## Local References and Project Portfolio

## Storm Damage and Insurance Claim Expertise

North Dakota sees severe hail and straight-line wind events nearly every summer. Filing a roof insurance claim correctly requires documentation most homeowners aren't trained to produce: dated photos, measured damage maps, and ITEL or matching-shingle reports.

Highpoint Construction works directly with adjusters from every major carrier serving North Dakota. We meet the adjuster on-site, document damage to industry standards, and handle supplements when the initial scope misses items. ## Safety Standards and Worker Protections

OSHA requires fall protection any time a roofer works six feet or more above a lower level. The NRCA reinforces this with mandatory harness use, anchor points, and toe-board systems on steep slopes.

Highpoint Construction crews use OSHA-compliant fall protection on every job, carry workers' comp on every employee, and never subcontract to uninsured day labor. Your homeowner's policy is not designed to pay for an injured roofer — ours is.
