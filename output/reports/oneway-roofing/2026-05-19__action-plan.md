## What to Look for When Hiring a Roofing Contractor

Hiring the wrong roofer is one of the most expensive mistakes a Houston homeowner can make. Use this checklist before you sign any contract — including one with Oneway Construction and Roofing.

- **Texas license and registration.** Texas does not require a state roofing license, but reputable contractors register with the Texas Roofing Contractors Association (RCAT) and carry a TRCC credential. Ask for the number in writing.
- **General liability and workers' comp insurance.** Request current certificates. The Texas Department of Insurance recommends verifying coverage directly with the carrier before work begins.
- **Local physical address in the Houston area.** Storm-chasers operate from out of state. A real Houston office means warranty support after the job ends.
- **Manufacturer certifications.** Look for GAF Master Elite, Owens Corning Preferred, or CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster status. These require background checks and training.
- **BBB accreditation and written reviews.** Check the Better Business Bureau profile and read recent Google reviews from Houston-area customers.
- **Written, line-item estimates.** The National Roofing Contractors Association (NRCA) advises against any contractor who refuses to itemize materials, labor, tear-off, and disposal.
- **Code compliance with IRC 2024 Chapter 9.** Your new roof must meet current International Residential Code requirements for underlayment, fastening, and ventilation.
- **Insurance claim experience.** If you have storm or hail damage, ask whether the contractor regularly works with State Farm, Allstate, USAA, and other major carriers.
- **Workmanship warranty in writing.** A minimum of 5 years on labor is standard; Oneway Construction and Roofing offers longer coverage on qualifying replacements.

Oneway Construction and Roofing meets every item on this list. [Contact our Houston team](https://onewayroof.com/contact/) to verify credentials before you decide.
