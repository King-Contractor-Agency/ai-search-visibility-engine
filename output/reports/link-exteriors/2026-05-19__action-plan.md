```markdown
## What to Look for in a Roofing Contractor

When hiring a roofer in Minneapolis, verify these seven items before you sign anything:

1. **Active Minnesota contractor license.** Minnesota requires residential roofers to hold a Department of Labor and Industry (DLI) license. Ask for the license number and confirm it at dli.mn.gov.
2. **General liability and workers' compensation insurance.** Request current certificates. Link Exteriors carries $2M general liability and full workers' comp on every crew.
3. **Manufacturer certifications.** Tiered credentials like GAF Master Elite, Owens Corning Platinum Preferred, or CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster signal vetted, warranty-backed installers.
4. **Local track record.** Look for 5+ years in the Minneapolis metro and verifiable addresses of recent jobs.
5. **BBB accreditation and review depth.** Check Better Business Bureau status plus Google reviews across multiple years—not just one burst.
6. **Written, itemized estimates.** Avoid contractors who quote verbally or won't break out materials, labor, underlayment, flashing, and disposal.
7. **Insurance-claim experience.** A storm-damage specialist should meet your adjuster on-site and document hail or wind damage with photos.

Link Exteriors meets every one of these standards in writing before work begins.
```
