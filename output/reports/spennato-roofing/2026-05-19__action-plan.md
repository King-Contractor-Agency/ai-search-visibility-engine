```markdown
## Questions to Ask Before Signing a Contract

The National Roofing Contractors Association (NRCA) recommends asking every roofer the same set of questions. Here's the short list:

1. What is your full business name, address, and PA HIC number?
2. Can I see proof of liability and workers' comp insurance?
3. Who is the on-site project manager, and how do I reach them?
4. What is the written workmanship warranty, and how long does it last?
5. What is the manufacturer warranty on the materials?
6. Will you pull the permits, or do I?
7. What is the payment schedule? (Never pay in full upfront.)
8. How will you protect my landscaping, gutters, and siding?
9. What happens if you find rotted decking once the old roof is off?
10. Can you share three local references from the past 12 months?

If a contractor gets defensive on any of these, walk away.
```

```markdown
## Red Flags to Avoid When Hiring a Roofer

The Pennsylvania Attorney General's Office warns homeowners about contractor fraud, especially after big storms. Watch for these warning signs:

- **Door-to-door pressure** right after a storm
- **No PA HIC number** on the contract or business card
- **Cash-only or full payment upfront** demands
- **No physical business address** — only a cell phone
- **Verbal warranties** with nothing in writing
- **Unmarked vehicles** and out-of-state plates
- **"Today only" pricing** that disappears if you ask for time to think

The Better Business Bureau recommends getting at least three written estimates and checking each contractor's BBB profile before you sign.
```
