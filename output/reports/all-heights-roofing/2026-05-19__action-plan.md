## Cleveland's Unique Roofing Challenges

Cleveland sits in one of the snowiest metro areas in the United States. According to National Weather Service Cleveland climate data, the city averages 60+ inches of snow per year, with lake-effect bands dropping 6 inches in a single afternoon. That creates four common roof problems we see week after week:

1. **Ice dams** at the eaves from poor attic insulation and ventilation.
2. **Granule loss** on south- and west-facing slopes from summer heat and UV.
3. **Wind-lifted shingles** from sustained 40+ mph gusts off Lake Erie.
4. **Hail bruising** that does not leak right away but fails 1–3 years later.

Every roof we install in Cleveland is built to current Ohio Building Code residential roofing requirements, including ice-and-water shield at all eaves, valleys, and penetrations — not just the minimum.
